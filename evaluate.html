Assessment of GHG emissions and cumulative excess above the base level

The assessment of an emitter’s GHG emissions is based on the use of the international ISO standard and involves determining the balance of emissions/removals generated by sources owned or controlled by the emitter (direct emissions) for a certain period, usually a year or a number of years.

Cumulative emissions excess is the sum of annual excesses of the base (2021) emissions level for the period from the base year to the year preceding the current one. If this value is greater than or equal to zero, the emitter has the right to register the defined tons of de-emission (DTD) and carbon units (CU) received from climate projects it implements.

Carbon Footprint Evaluation

An emitter's carbon footprint represents the sum of its direct emissions and indirect emissions resulting from the production and transportation of the main (most significant) types of materials and energy it consumes. In accordance with the principles of the Initiative, the emitter's carbon footprint does not include emissions from its products, which are included in the area of responsibility (carbon footprint) of consumers of these products.

We make it possible to assess the carbon footprint of the emitter:

  • for individuals, households, small (up to 100 employees) businesses, if the latter do not include large sources of GHG emissions - by meeting the Initiative's benchmarks (25 tons of CO2 emission per person annually).
  • for legal entities, medium and large enterprises using specialized computer programs and services of professional organizations

The carbon footprint of products consumed by the emitter (input carbon footprint) is taken into account when determining the mass of indirect emissions included in the emitter's carbon footprint.

The carbon footprint of products manufactured by the emitter is counted to confirm the carbon neutrality of these products, which can help increase attractiveness of these products to consumers.

Assessment of the results of climate
Projects and their verification
Assessment of the climate efficiency of projects implemented by emitters involves determining GHG emissions mass reduction (prevention), their capture and storage, and increasing the absorption capacity of natural complexes. The assessment results can be presented as defined tons of de-emissions (DTD), and, if certain conditions are met, as carbon units (CU).

In determining the results of the action, the Initiative abandons assumption-based “baselines” and scenario approach. Calculations are based on actual data on a “before-and-now” basis, taking into account the base level emissions or the first year in which the actual effect of the action was achieved.

Based on the results of this type of assessment, the Initiative Registry can issue DTDs and CUs and enter them into the owners' accounts in the Initiative's carbon Registry.

A partner professional organization can also provide services for re-verification of previously issued CUs assessed by other professional organizations. The goal is to eliminate double counting, climate fakes, and greenwashing. Cases of incorrect accounting of emissions quickly returning to the atmosphere, leaks of emissions to other enterprises, industries and regions are also taken into account and excluded.
