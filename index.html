The Initiative
what it is about

The main task is to reform carbon markets and correctly register the results of de-emissions - actions to reduce (prevent) greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, their capture and disposal, and increase the absorption capacity of natural complexes.

Our Manifesto calls for the transformation of the fight against global climate change from a scientific and bureaucratic undertaking into a task in which all inhabitants of the Earth are involved on the principles of equality.

Our efforts are aimed at changing people's lifestyles, ways of production, reorienting humanity towards economical consumption of resources while maintaining their usual way of life.

The initiative postulates three types of equality
  • Each ton of de-emission is equal to another, regardless of the type of project, industry, region or entity which assessed and verified it
  • Small businesses and households participate in the process of emissions reduction along with large corporations, states and their alliances.
  • Old CUs (before 2012), after they have been verified are taken into account along with new ones before a certain moment

Climate movement

Combat with global climate change has been going on for more than 30 years. Despite numerous conferences, efforts taken by some countries and large companies, serious results are yet to be reached. GHG content in the air increases by 0,5-0,7 % yearly. Total emissions increase by 350-500 million tons  every year   with the population growth. Per capita emissions of 6,6 thousand tons per year do not decrease.Many climate activists and the general public gather the  efforts are futile. But this situation can be changed.

By discarding the false concept of “net zero”,  reducing emissions by about 20 billion tons of CO2e per year, we can stabilize the GHG content in the Earth’s atmosphere and maintain existing climatic conditions, adaptation to which is not difficult. Thus, the problem of global climate change on Earth can be solved

De-emisson De-emission - implementation of measures leading to the reduction of emissions or GHGs removal from the atmosphere. The result of this process can be registered in defined tons of de-emission (DTD)
Cancellation A person wishing to offset own carbon footprint may acquire DCRT and cancel (burn) them, simultaneously annulling in the Registry the CUs that back those DCRTs
CU and Register CU is a Defined ton of de-emission, produced by an emitter that has covered its cumulative increase in emissions over the 2021 base year level, which can be used to offset the carbon footprint of other emitters. 1 CU= 1 DTD=1 ton of de-emission in CO2e
DCRT Digital Carbon Unit - DCRT - a derivative liquid marketable asset backed by CU stored in the Registry
De-emission - is a key concept of the Initiative.
De-emission is carried out by any person - natural or legal - in the process of implementing activities that lead to GHG emission reduction, removal , storage , improvement of absorbing capacity of natural complexes.
De-emission results have to be verified solely by the emitter or by designated professional organizations using specially designed software. De-emission measures implementation is expected to be declarative. Accounting is carried out in Defined tons of de-emission (DTD), where 1 DTD=1 ton CO2e is confirmed by emitter’s assessment and verified by the Initiative’s adopted procedure.
DTD can be recorded as Carbon Units (CU) if annual accumulated emissions excess of the Emitter,who got DTDs, since base year till the year preceding current, is offset. CUs can be used for sale or exchange and is eligible to offset carbon footprint of other emitters.

Carbon Units and DTD are accounted in The Initiative Carbon Registry.
Currently the Registry contains 1000 million tons of DTD/CU
CU stored in the Registry back digital carbon units - DCRT. Thus DCRT is a derivative of CU available for sale, stored in the registry. DCRT is marketable asset with great usability.
As DCRT are backed by real carbon units - tons of CO2e de-emission - they can be canceled (burnt) to offset emitter’s carbon footprint. As DCRT s are “burnt” they are transferred to special purses that can not be accessed and same amount of CUs is  canceled - i.e..get deregistered for good. Millions of CUs in the registry are eligible for emitters who would like to offset carbon footprint - own, of goods they produce or mankind’s.
The Initiative issues NFT certificates in favor of the emitters, confirming carbon footprint offset in in tCO2e and relative to emitter’s total carbon footprint
Emission and
carbon footprint
assessment
We offer to assess carbon footprint:
  • for individuals, households, small enterprises if the latter do not emit significant amount of GHG based on Initiative’s reference point of 25 tons CO2e of emission per capita annually
  • for legal entities using services of professional organizations
  • after verification (re-verification), the assessment of emission and carbon footprint obtained earlier by emitters can be taken into account

The Initiative, at the request of the Client, with the participation of partner professional organizations (PPOs), provides the following assessment services

  • the mass of the issuer's emissions for a certain period
  • the emitter’'s carbon footprint for the year or since the beginning of the base period (2021)
  • manufactured products carbon footprint
  • cumulative annual increase in emissions for the period since base year of 2021 to year preceding current one
  • results of de-emission measures in tons of CO2e, assistance in registering and obtaining DTD and CU

Initiative's carbon registry
The Carbon Equality Initiative Carbon Registry is a standardized electronic database that provides registration, release, storage, receipt, transfer, acquisition, cancellation and withdrawal of carbon units (CU), as well as defined tons of de-emissions (DTD), registration of DCRT emissions, climate projects, and also low-carbon technologies, de-emission methods and best practices supported by the Initiative.
The registry provides the following services:
  • registers and enters into your account in the Registry the defined tons of de-emission (DTD) and carbon units (CU) following the climate projects you have implemented
  • stores your CUs and carries out transactions with them at your instructions
  • long term storage of documentation and DTD and CU issuance legality confirmations
  • converts DTD into CU upon receipt of appropriate conclusion.
  • provides you with digital carbon units (DCU) DCRT, subject to the transfer of the corresponding number of CUs belonging to you to the Initiative Reserve
  • transfers to your account CUs held in the Reserve in exchange for DCRT belonging to you
  • Cancellation (burning) of DCRT owned by you in order to offset your, or your product carbon footprint
Besides, the Registry, with the participation of the initiative, registers projects intended or implemented by you, as well as low-carbon technologies, methods of GHG de-emission, and best practices proposed by you as endorsed (recommended) by the Initiative.
